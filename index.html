Understanding Changes in Corporate Compliance Regulations

Recent legislative changes impact how businesses must approach compliance. Learn what you need to know to protect your company.

In 2023, federal and state regulators introduced sweeping reforms to corporate compliance frameworks, particularly targeting financial transparency, data privacy, and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) disclosures. Companies failing to adapt may face steep penalties, reputational damage, or even loss of operating licenses.

Key updates include:

  • Mandatory annual compliance audits for firms with over 50 employees.
  • New whistleblower protections and anonymous reporting channels.
  • Stricter penalties for non-compliance — up to 4% of global revenue.
  • Board-level accountability for compliance program effectiveness.

Our legal team recommends conducting a compliance gap analysis immediately and updating internal policies before the Q4 enforcement wave. Schedule a consultation with our compliance specialists to ensure your business remains protected.

Navigating Child Custody During Divorce

Key considerations for parents facing custody decisions and strategies for protecting your children's best interests.

Child custody battles can be emotionally draining and legally complex. Courts prioritize the "best interests of the child," which involves evaluating parental stability, emotional bonds, living conditions, and each parent's willingness to support the child's relationship with the other parent.

Important factors courts consider:

  • Each parent's mental and physical health.
  • The child's adjustment to home, school, and community.
  • History of domestic violence or substance abuse.
  • The child's own preference (if age 12 or older in many states).

We help parents develop parenting plans that are fair, enforceable, and child-centered. Mediation is often encouraged to reduce conflict — our attorneys are skilled negotiators and fierce litigators when needed.

The Importance of Proper Estate Planning

Why everyone needs an estate plan, regardless of age or wealth, and the essential documents to protect your legacy.

Estate planning isn't just for the wealthy — it's for anyone who wants to protect their loved ones and ensure their wishes are honored. Without a plan, state laws decide who inherits your assets, which may not align with your intentions.

Essential documents include:

  • Will – Specifies how assets are distributed and names guardians for minor children.
  • Living Trust – Avoids probate and offers privacy and control over asset distribution.
  • Power of Attorney – Appoints someone to manage finances if you become incapacitated.
  • Advance Healthcare Directive – Outlines medical wishes and appoints a healthcare proxy.

Life events like marriage, divorce, birth of a child, or acquiring significant assets should trigger a review of your estate plan. Our attorneys tailor plans to your unique family and financial situation.
